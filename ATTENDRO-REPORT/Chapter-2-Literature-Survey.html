CHAPTER 2
LITERATURE SURVEY

In the context of academic integrity and administrative efficiency, attendance management remains a critical function. This chapter reviews the evolution of attendance systems, from manual registers to advanced biometric and IoT-based solutions. It analyzes existing technologies to identify gaps and formulates the Problem Statement that the Attendro system addresses.

2.1 Review of Existing Systems

Attendance systems have evolved significantly over the years. The existing methodologies can be broadly categorized into Manual Systems, RFID/Smart Card Systems, and Biometric Systems.

2.1.1 Manual Attendance Systems

The traditional method involves roll calls or signing a paper registry. While simple to implement, this method is fraught with inefficiencies. It consumes valuable lecture time, creates distracting interruptions, and is legally prone to manipulation and "proxy" attendance, where students sign in for absent peers.

2.1.2 Radio Frequency Identification (RFID) Systems

RFID systems introduce automation by asking students to tap ID cards against a reader. While faster than manual calls, RFID systems fundamentally identify the card, not the student. It is trivial for one student to carry multiple cards and mark attendance for friends, failing to solve the core issue of integrity.

2.1.3 Traditional Biometric Systems

Fingerprint and iris scanners provide high security by verifying biological traits. Ross & Jain (2021) highlight their accuracy in high-security environments. However, in an educational setting, queuing an entire class to touch a single sensor creates bottlenecks and hygiene concerns. Furthermore, most deployed biometric units are "standalone" devices that lack awareness of the complex, changing academic schedule.

2.1.4 Mobile App-based Systems

Recent solutions utilize students' own smartphones via GPS or Bluetooth. While convenient, GPS is often too inaccurate for indoor tracking (distinguishing between adjacent classrooms), and location spoofing apps are readily available.

2.2 Gap Analysis

The literature survey reveals a distinct gap between integrity and efficiency. Generally, systems that are secure (like biometrics) are slow and intrusive, while systems that are fast (like RFID or Apps) are insecure or prone to spoofing.

Furthermore, most hardware solutions are "context-unaware." A standard fingerprint scanner records who and when, but has no knowledge of which lecture is currently scheduled, requiring tedious manual reconciliation of logs by administrators.

2.3 Problem Statement

The Core Problem: Educational institutions lack a unified attendance system that ensures high integrity (no proxies) without disrupting the flow of education. Current solutions force a trade-off between security and speed, and often operate in silos disconnected from the academic timetable.

Specific Challenges Identified:

  1. The Proxy Problem: Inability to verify the physical presence of the actual student reliably.
  2. The Data Silo Problem: Hardware devices do not communicate in real-time with student management software.
  3. The Bottleneck Problem: High-security checks take too long for large classes.

Defined Goal: To design and implement "Attendro," a system that utilizes IoT-enabled biometric hardware coupled with a smart cloud platform. The system must verify identity biometrically to eliminate proxies, sync in real-time to provide "live" class monitoring, and integrate seamlessly with the academic schedule to automate the entire attendance lifecycle.
